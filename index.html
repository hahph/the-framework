01

Business Challenges

At 16% annual attrition — above the benchmark — the organization was losing talent faster than it could retain.
Yet the more pressing problem was not the rate itself — it was that no one could explain what was driving it.

Talent Sustainability Gap

Attrition Without a Traceable Cause

Turnover remained above benchmark, yet no mechanism existed to determine whether it was driven by structural misalignment, compensation gaps, career ceiling, or market pull. Without distinguishing the root cause, any intervention risked treating the symptom.

Risk: Misdiagnosing an attrition driver doesn't just waste retention spend — it accelerates the erosion of institutional knowledge while weakening employer brand

Diagnostic Segmentation Gap

No Profile of Who Is Leaving

Aggregate attrition figures were never broken down by segments — leaving the organization unable to determine whether departures were evenly distributed or clustered within a group of employees whose loss carried outsized impact.

Risk: Retention resources spread uniformly across the workforce protect everyone in theory — but a concentrated problem demands a targeted response

HR Capacity Gap

The Solving Team Is Breaking Down

As annual departures across the organization exceeded 700, the HR department faced mounting recruitment and onboarding demand — while simultaneously experiencing its own elevated attrition at 30%, the highest rate across all departments.

Risk: The function responsible for stabilizing workforce turnover was itself in crisis, which resulted in the organization's capacity to recover being structurally compromised

Executive Decision Gap

Data Present, Informed Decisions Absent

Workforce data existed across systems, but no analytical framework synthesized it into a coherent picture of underlying causes. The gap between raw data and actionable insights remained unbridged — leaving leadership without a solid basis for decision-making.

Risk: The data remains descriptive rather than prescriptive, and retention strategies default to intuition rather than evidence

02

My Role & Ownership

The starting point was not the dataset, but the business question: Why is this organization losing people, and What would it take to stop it?
Every analytical decision was made independently, iterated through multiple versions, and refined until the findings could stand on their own.

01 / Framing

Problem Definition

Framed the business problem as the analytical starting point — identifying what leadership would need to understand, what decisions the analysis should enable, and what questions the dataset would need to answer.

02 / Planning

Analytical Blueprint

Defined target stakeholders (CEO, Board, CHRO), mapped the key questions and metrics the report would address, and produced a structural mockup — establishing the analytical intent before building began.

03 / Preparation

Data Foundation

Connected the dataset to Power BI, applied data cleaning and transformations with Power Query, and built the underlying semantic model — establishing the relational structure needed to support multi-dimensional analysis.

04 / Investigation

Exploratory Analysis

Conducted iterative data exploration to surface patterns beneath the aggregate numbers — analyzing attrition across multiple dimensions and identifying the segmental findings that became the analytical core of the report.

05 / Build

Report Development

Translated analytical findings into a structured report using data storytelling principles — designing the layout, selecting chart types for each page, and refining through multiple iterations until ready for a leadership audience.

06 / Manage

Distribution & Access

Published the report to Power BI Service and configured it for ongoing use — applying row-level security for access control, setting up scheduled data refresh, and enabling self-service analytics within predefined permissions.

The report was structured to narrow toward root cause — answering one class of business question before the next.
Power BI was chosen for its capacity to support multi-dimensional analysis across large datasets and deliver self-service analytics that leadership can interrogate independently.

The Report

A walkthrough of the report as built — six analytical pages, each answering one class of business question in sequence.
Together they trace the path from systemic observation to structural finding to executive action framework.

01 / Executive Layer
The Decision Brief

Four key cards frame the workforce stakes — The challenges, Business Impacts, Key Findings, and Solution Preview — giving leadership a 60-second read of the full situation before any deeper investigation begins.

Page 01 — Executive Summary
02 / Descriptive Layer
Workforce Profile

Satisfaction scores, compensation patterns, demographic distribution, and working hour profiles establish the corporate context — setting the baseline against which all segment-level findings are measured and confirming that attrition is systemic.

Page 02 — Workforce Profile
03 / Diagnostic Layer
Hot Spot Mapping

Segmentation across department, job level, role, and age progressively isolates the high-risk population — narrowing from 711 total departures to the 312-employee cluster that accounts for 44% of annual attrition and defines the priority focus area.

Page 03 — Volume Analysis
04 / Investigative Layer
The Dead Angle

Cross-dimensional analysis revealed the patterns that department-level metrics obscure — including hidden drivers of departure that aggregate figures cannot surface and that only become visible through multi-factor examination.

Page 04 — Rate Analysis
05 / Causal Layer
The Structural Finding

Organizational misalignment — 711 staff classified in departments misaligned with their functional roles — is validated as the primary driver, with the correlation between misclassified headcount and annual departure volume forming the structural evidence base.

Page 05 — Root Cause
06 / Prescriptive Layer
Intervention Roadmap

Analytical findings are translated into a three-phase intervention roadmap — with modeled cost trajectory, targeted investment sizing, and projected return structured for executive review and resource allocation decisions at Board level.

Business Value Delivered

The analysis moved beyond surface-level reporting to expose the structural and segmental patterns underneath — giving leadership
the diagnostic clarity needed to intervene with precision rather than assumption.

✔ Causal Clarity

From Rate to Root Cause

Attrition was traced beyond the headline number to its structural origin — organizational misalignment affecting 711 employees, pointing to a fixable structural cause rather than the cultural or compensation drivers typically assumed.

✔ Targeted Visibility

Concentration Mapped, Intervention Enabled

Workforce attrition was not evenly distributed. Analysis surfaced 312 employees — 7% of the workforce — accounting for 44% of annual departures, enabling retention resources to be directed where they carry the highest return.

✔ Diagnostic Integrity

The Real Signal, Separated from the Noise

The 30% HR turnover rate — read as crisis — was reframed as a structural misclassification. Proper realignment projected HR attrition returning to the organizational baseline, removing the false signal and redirecting response toward the actual drivers of departure.

✔ Decision Readiness

Analysis Structured for Executive Action

Findings were translated into a phased intervention roadmap — with modeled cost exposure ($33M baseline), illustrative investment sizing ($22M), and projected ROI (37.7%) designed to drive strategic capital allocation at the executive level.
